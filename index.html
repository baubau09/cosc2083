Project Overview

 
  • Topic
  • Motivation
  • Landscape

This project will be a movie streaming platform called RECO, where users can not only watch a selection of movies in high definition but also share their real-time experience with others within the same session. Reco will focus on the socializing aspect of the watching movies activity, which is extremely lacking with the current pandemic situation, in Vietnam as well as the world. Beside allowing the user to watch movies, the app will also have a built-in recommending system, in which users can pre-define their “taste” in movies, allowing the system to find and recommend the most suitable movies to the user through learning algorithm. This will ensure that the user will have the best experience while using the app, alone or with others.

Reco is currently being proposed as a mobile app, but if it was developed successfully, the team may even consider bringing it to other platforms, such as desktop or even smart TVs to allow a wider accessibility, further reinforcing the idea of connecting people through Reco. It will be a new way for people to meet and make friends, similar to how social media works, not just during the pandemic, but also for our normal day to day life.

It has been a long time since the COVID-19 pandemic first appeared which led to huge changes in citizens’ lives. Connection between people is affected obviously during the continuous lockdown since people cannot have meetings normally like they did before. Consequently, it is becoming more difficult for citizens to share their hobbies and interests with each other. The act of sharing by texting through technical devices is much different from face-to-face experience. The motivation to create this app is helping people to share their hobbies in their free time such as watching movies together. Movie theaters have been closed for 1 year due to social distancing so it must be a while since people could watch movies together with high quality. Sharing screens to watch movies together through Discord, Zoom, etc. reduces the quality of the movies inevitably.

This app allows people to watch movies together with better quality. Additionally, people can share their interest in movies with each other by discussing it while watching it together without being afraid of annoying other people like in movie theaters. This is the key to maintaining the connection between human beings during COVID-19 situation. Besides, our app has a filter to pick a movie by filtering genres that people in the same group like to watch. It helps them to find a suitable movie that includes genres that all people in the same group are into watching so they can watch it together. There is no certain claim that this pandemic will end soon. Therefore, our motivation is to help people have a great time-sharing hobby with each other such as watching movies together since citizens cannot go out normally like they did before.

The concept of having a synchronized watch sessions between devices and users has been done before by Teleparty, a web browser extension. However, unlike Teleparty, Reco will use this concept and develop upon it with additional features, making it a standalone app. This will make Reco less prone to bugs and errors, as well as opening up opportunity for the team to expand on the idea in future iterations of the app. Furthermore, Reco will have a much wider interact options for its users, such as the implementation of friend list, setting schedules for a watch session, or even browse a selection of watch rooms created by others.

Aims

The Main Aim: Making RECO

RECO is the focal point of our team. The team has identified a number of problems in the world at the moment as mentioned in the Overview section. To address those problems, we came up with RECO, a system where people around the world can connect and enjoy their favorite movies together. To fulfill this aim, we would need to achieve the following goals:

  • Goal 1: Creating a fitting UI

    The User Interface is the first thing that every potential user looks at when they consider using an application or not. Because of this, it is important that the interface of RECO is interesting and intuitive. For a movie related application like RECO, this could be something like a darker theme to simulate a movie theatre room, or a clean and fully functioning video control panel.

  • Goal 2: The Recommendation Algorithm

    The trouble of choosing a fitting movie for you and your friends is a common problem nowadays. The team addresses this problem by having a learning algorithm that uses a user’s watching patterns and prior movie choices to select the most enjoyable movie for that individual. This will ensure the user will have the best experience using RECO and not wasting time selecting a movie for a session.

  • Goal 3: The Social Aspect of a Streaming Platform

    The biggest goal of our project is to connect people together through their interest in movies, so it is important that we can provide the best social experience to our users. RECO will not only be a place where people can watch movies, it is where people can share that experience with their friends and family. To achieve this, the team will have to do research on movie streaming platforms along side with social media sites. Friend list, chat room, face cam, are some of the things that will be utilized in RECO to emphasize on this aspect of the system.

  • Goal 4: Earning Profit and Experience

    Just like any business projects, the development of RECO should bring back a reward for the team beside solving the identified problems. Throughout the development, team members will learn a lot of new things regarding the software development life cycle. Especially for RECO, where many innovating IT concepts are being implemented such as machine learning algorithm or management of servers. After that, if the release of RECO was a success, it could also mean profit for the team through different methods of monetization.

Scope and Limits

Understanding our own ideas and the requirements for the project, the in-scope deliverables, and the out-of-scope features are concluded as follows:

  • In scope

    • High-priority features:

        User authentication: The user must login to use the application

        Sign-up and onboarding: When finished signing up and login in for the first, the user will be guided through an onboarding flow, where the system will learn the user’s initial preferences, and connect the user with their current streaming platforms.

        Bottom navigation bar: A general menu for navigating between different sections including Home, Liked, Rooms, Browse, and Invitation

        Home: the heart of the application, with an unparalleled card-swiping experience to like/dislike a move or TV show

        Liked: contains all the movies/TV shows that the user has swiped right. The user can also search, and filter liked movies

        Rooms system: a rooming system for users of the application to watch movies in different rooms with extended features

        Rooms menu: show all the rooms the user created and joined

        Create a room: this button will appear in 2 places (movie details and when there is a match, but no rooms have been created). In the new room pop-up dialog, the movie title is automatically filled beforehand when we click on the button. The user only has to fill in Room Name, Time, and Date to watch, and select the people to add to the room.

        Room details: the main feature that will account for 70% of the screen is Chat, where users can discuss that movie. The rest of the screen is reserved for displaying important information about the movie including

             Movie title

             Date and time to watch

             A button to copy shareable link for quick invitation

             A button “Watch now” to watch (this button is only enabled at watch time)

             If the user is an admin of the room, there is a setting button to change the room details and the people in the room

        Watch together: a video call with the actual movie to watch, and movie controls (room admin only), there is also a chat interface if clicked on the Chat button

        Room user role system: there are 2 types of roles in a room, admin, and member. Admin can control the movie (play, pause, forward) as well as the room settings. Member can watch and initiate a “pause request” when they need to go somewhere and wish to temporarily pause the movie.

    • Medium-priority features:

        Browse: the user can browse through recommendations, search, and filter movies

        Invitation: shows all invitations to watch

        Movie details: shows movie’s title, director, genres, summary, cast, length, match percentage, IMDB score, duration, and the number of friends who liked the movie. There is a button to create a room for this movie.

    • Low-priority features:

        Top app bar: A supplementary menu for quickly accessing User profile settings and Notification, along with RECO’s logo

        User profile settings:

          See all friends

          Edit profile details

          Edit settings

        Notification:

          Notify the user when there is a match

          Notify the user when there is a new invitation to watch

          Notify the user when there are new friend requests, and when friend requests are accepted

          Alert the user of incoming watch schedule

  • Out of scope

    This project does not include a content management system, where administrators, contributors, or content creators have a front-end to freely upload new movies or TV shows. Moreover, the application does not support direct streaming as it is not a streaming service itself. It can only fetch data to watch when the user has already connected to their streaming platform like Netflix, FPT Play or VieON. It also does not allow users to rate movies on a 5-point scale system.

Testing

Early Development:
Before any programming in this project, the team will need to know the requirements for the app itself, meaning what RECO will do and how the users use RECO. It is important that this step is done thoroughly to avoid increasing testing and debugging cost in the future.
We can do this by giving out surveys to our target market. An early mockup will be made based on the requirements and given to selected groups. In this case, it is most convenient to choose students within RMIT, or relatives of project members due to the pandemic situation. This way, we can get early feedback on different aspects such as UI/UX, software features, etc.

During Development:
After gathering requirements, we will begin the programming phase. Here, programmers will write modules for the software. It is the programmer’s responsibility to make sure the functions and procedures within the module are working properly through white box and black box testing. Interactions between modules must also be tested by the developers to make the system can work together as a whole. This process can be done faster by using a good IDE for development, which can provide debugging tools such as syntax highlight, error highlight, executing selected codes.
Various kinds of tools can be used for efficient testing in this phase. Trello or similar tools can be used to create a checklist of test cases, allowing the tester to go through each case and know what parameters to pass. Additionally, GitHub is also a great tool for version control, in which if an error is found in one iteration, we can roll back to a previous functioning version.

After Development:
RECO should be fully functional software at this stage; however, we can further test it to ensure it is truly ready for deployment. An Alpha test version is released among project’s members, those who know the internal functions of the system. If any bugs were encountered, the team can fix it immediately. This process can be time-consuming, but it is needed before we can release Beta version. The Beta will be given to RMIT students for testing and feedback. The reason we choose RMIT students is because they have diverse backgrounds and domain knowledge, which we believe is essential to provide us with constructive criticisms. Any feedback and bugs found with the beta version will be compiled and evaluate in the next iteration of RECO.

Time frame

We intend to use the API-first development strategy. This approach makes future expansion to the application efficient. Overall, we plan to develop essential APIs first, then creating the application and testing in chain for about seven weeks, followed by internal testing and debugging for four weeks. Finally, we will release the beta version for user test during week 16 while planning for the web application expansion.

There are many risks when it comes to software development; no projects are immune from risk. Therefore, without identifying clearly as many risks as possible before the project, it will increase the chance of failing. This section will cover some of the risks that this project may encounter during and after development:

Copyright and Ownership Issues – One of the biggest concerns when creating RECO is that the team does not own any movies, but rather we will partner with other streaming platforms such as Netflix or HBO Max to stream their movies. Because of this, there will need to be an agreement between the team and those streaming companies to allow us to include their contents in RECO. It will also require the user to own the subscription to a specific streaming service to be able to watch it on our app.

User Interface Implementation – The current team has limited knowledge on the interface design aspect of software development. Because of this, we might not be able to fully implement every function with every component shown in the current UI/UX design. This will affect the final usability of our software when deploy to our customers, leading to a drop in user experience. To overcome this problem, the team might need to outsource to front-end developers and designers, which will affect the available budget of the project.

Limited Accessibility – RECO is currently being developed for a mobile platform, including mobile phones and tablets. Meaning people who want to use RECO on PC or smart TV would need to use a third-party software to do so. This will affect the number of potential users that RECO can have, especially since most people enjoy watching movies on a big monitor. The team wants to extend the working platform for RECO in the future if the current project succeeds.

Security – To use RECO, user will have to register an account, meaning their personal information will be stored on the team’s server. Since the software will also require to accessing user’s information from other services such as Netflix or Amazon, the security of RECO’s server needs to be put in high priority. Failing to secure user’s data in this case may lead to serious consequences.

Communication during Pandemic – With the current situation of COVID-19, the team is limited in term of communication. Without a face-to-face meeting, it might be difficult for the team to convey ideas from one to another, which may lead to misunderstanding during requirement gathering and developing. Not only for internal communication, acquiring external services will also be a challenge that requires more time and resources.

Group processes and communications

Our daily communication is via a created group on Team and Messenger on Facebook. We chose Team since it is more convenient to find members through student id at first. Discussion about the assignment and brainstorming ideas are through Team because voice-to-voice meetings are more connecting which make it more productive and easier to develop the assignment. When there was not important enough to have meetings on Team, Messenger was chosen as it is common social media in Vietnam which we all use it day-to-day, we found it easier to chat with each other through Messenger whenever we need to announce the stages of assignment process. One member in our group created it after we found teammates on week 8. Our meetings are available twice a week from week 9.

Although all the members were busy with last week's assignments from different courses, they were still highly active in communicating with the team to work on the project. Therefore, there was no case that a group member did not respond to the communications. However, if this situation happened, the other members would try to remind that member about their responsibility politely. After that, we would give them the tasks that belong to their responsibility in the assignment and give them the deadlines that everyone in the group already discussed before. We would check that member’s process two days before the deadlines just in case that member did not take his/her responsibility. If he/she were in the process and finished their tasks before the deadlines, everything would be fine as there were no obstacles in our process. In contrast, if he/she ignored his/her responsibility, we would do it ourselves to complete the tasks on deadlines and will talk with the lecturer about the situation later. We would ask the lecturer to consider the situation and to give the scores fairly since we are all adults, and we must be responsible for what we have done.

Our communication outside the class happened frequently on Messengers since we need to announce the stages of our process, about two to four times a week.
